# Media Coverage — Notes (headline / outlet / date / summary only, no full-text scrapes)

## 1. MIT Technology Review
- **Headline:** "Google DeepMind's new AI assistant helps elite soccer coaches get even better"
- **Author:** Rhiannon Williams
- **Publication:** MIT Technology Review
- **Date:** March 19, 2024
- **URL:** https://www.technologyreview.com/2024/03/19/1089927/google-deepminds-new-ai-assistant-helps-elite-soccer-coaches-get-even-better/
- **Summary:** Reports on TacticAI's launch alongside the Nature Communications paper. Explains that the system converts corner-kick scenarios into graphs and uses predictive + generative models to recommend player positioning, predicting outcomes like shot attempts and first-touch likelihood. Notes that Liverpool FC experts preferred TacticAI's suggestions over real tactics 90% of the time and often couldn't distinguish AI-generated setups from real ones. Mentions researchers' belief the approach could extend to other set pieces, open play, and other sports (basketball, hockey).

## 2. SportsPro
- **Headline:** "Google DeepMind creates AI tool to help Liverpool score more corner kicks"
- **Author:** Steve McCaskill
- **Publication:** SportsPro
- **Date:** March 21, 2024
- **URL:** https://www.sportspro.com/news/google-deepmind-gen-ai-analytics-liverpool/
- **Summary:** Covers the TacticAI announcement from a sports-business/sports-tech angle. Describes the predictive/generative capabilities (likely receiver, shot probability, historical comparison to similar tactics) and the 90%-preference finding from Liverpool's coaching staff. Frames TacticAI as an extension of a three-year-plus DeepMind–Liverpool FC collaboration on AI for sports analytics and player recruitment.

## 3. The Register
- **Headline:** "Back of the net? Google's DeepMind is coming for football tactics"
- **Author:** Lindsay Clark
- **Publication:** The Register
- **Date:** March 20, 2024
- **URL:** https://www.theregister.com/2024/03/20/googles_deepmind_football/
- **Summary:** A more skeptical/tech-press treatment of the same announcement. Notes TacticAI was trained on data from over 7,000 corner-kick plays, can predict ball receivers and outcomes, and generates alternative setups experts rated favorably 90% of the time. Speculates the tool could see wider adoption by teams preparing for the 2026 FIFA World Cup in the United States.

## 4. TechMyMoney — Palmeiras deployment (2026 follow-on development)
- **Headline:** "Google DeepMind Brings TacticAI Football Predictions to Palmeiras"
- **Publication:** TechMyMoney
- **Date:** June 13, 2026
- **URL:** https://techmymoney.com/2026/06/13/google-deepmind-tacticai-palmeiras-football/
- **Summary:** Reports that on June 11, 2026, Google DeepMind announced Brazilian club Palmeiras (in partnership with the Confederação Brasileira de Futebol, CBF) as the first club to "meaningfully build upon" TacticAI, unveiled at the Google for Brazil event. Describes an extended version of the system that reportedly works from broadcast-style visual data (rather than the original corner-kick tracking-data setup) and can forecast open-play dynamics up to 8 seconds into the future, going beyond the original corner-kick-only scope of the peer-reviewed 2024 work.
