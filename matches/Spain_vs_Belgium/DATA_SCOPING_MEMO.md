## 4. Creative alternative-data angles (with fool-me risk flagged)

1. **Reuse the campaign's early-vs-late hydration-break timing split directly for Q5/Q6.** `KNOCKOUT_STAGE_PRICING_DEEP_DIVE.md` (a 4-match, campaign-wide audit, independent of any single match) found: early-window questions (before 1st hydration break) went **+30.86 RBP across 3/3 positive instances**; late-window questions (after 2nd hydration break) went **-45.90 RBP across 3/3 negative instances**, with the explicit lesson to shade any post-second-hydration-break question toward 0.45-0.55 regardless of how clean the underlying team signal looks, because late-match state (fatigue, subs, scoreline management) swamps pre-match averages. A more specific, single-match version of this same pattern was independently reconfirmed in the France-Morocco case study (goal-timing overall +25.93, card-after-hydration-break -43.19). **Risk of being fooled:** both are still small-sample audits (3-4 instances each) of a question *type*, not this specific matchup — treat as a shading prior, not a hard rule, and don't stack the two sources as if they were independent evidence (they overlap in mechanism).
2. **Yamal's age/sample-size asymmetry vs De Bruyne/Lukaku's veteran depth is a real, project-validated structural signal, not just a narrative.** Yamal has zero major-tournament history before 2026 (StatsBomb panel confirms 0 rows) — his entire signal is 4 games of 2026 data, and that signal has already been validated once this exact tournament (Portugal-Spain: real score/assist rate ~25% vs crowd's 51%, market overrates his reputation). De Bruyne and Lukaku, by contrast, have 10 major-tournament StatsBomb rows each (2018+2022) that independently agree with their 2026 form (40% historical 1+SOT rate vs 2/4 and comparable-shot-volume-but-sub-role in 2026). **Risk of being fooled:** the *reason* Yamal's market price runs hot (reputation/hype as a teen superstar) is a real and recurring bias worth exploiting, but it is a single-player pattern reconfirmed on the same player in back-to-back matches — that's reinforcement, not independent replication, and it will eventually face a match where he does convert. Don't treat "3 for 3" as more than what it is: the same trade, taken a third time.
3. **Squad-value/age-profile asymmetry (Transfermarkt) as a "aging golden generation vs emerging golden generation" framing for the win-probability question (Q1) is available but untested and worth using cautiously.** `data/external/transfermarkt/transfermarkt-datasets/player_valuations.csv.gz` exists (last updated 2026-06-02) and could quantify a Spain-youth vs Belgium-veteran squad value/age gap; `data/external/fifa_ranking/fifa_ranking-2020-12-10.csv` also exists but is nearly 6 years stale and should not be used for a current strength read. **Risk of being fooled:** squad market value is a weak, slow-moving proxy for on-pitch strength in a single knockout match, and the historical head-to-head in `data/international_results/results.csv` (Spain won or drew nearly every meeting 2004-2016, most recently 2016) is too dated (10 years old, different player generations entirely) to carry real weight — mention it as context, not as a pricing input.
